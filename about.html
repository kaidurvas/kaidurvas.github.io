The Past Life

He is a scholar of forgotten things, drawn to the gaps in history where memory falters and myth begins. He observes the world through absence—what’s missing, what’s buried, what never had a name. His inquiry lives where perception ruptures—at the event horizon of thought.

This Life

Kai Durvas is an internationally unrecognized author. His works, described as “unskimmable” and “definitely a book”, have not yet been banned—but they have been underlined feverishly by at least one conspiracy theorist. He has no digital footprint and believes all dogs are good dogs. He trains for what cannot be fought, and writes what cannot be said.

Next Transmission

He writes in modern script but dreams in dead languages. His debut novel, Katabasis, is the first of three revelations—an unscrolling of mind, myth, and memory before the last thought dies. This is only the beginning.
