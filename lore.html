Phoibos: Builder of the First City

He did not build walls. He built rules. The world arranged itself around them.

The God Phoibos was the first builder of cities. He is the father of geometry and architecture—but what he built was never stone. It was strategy made stable. A game, sustained through time. And from that game, the first city arose.

From the players he studied, and from the game he loved, Phoibos derived the Seven Laws—four Roots and three Branches—that now govern every lasting city of the Empire. They are the secret architecture beneath all enduring cooperation.
